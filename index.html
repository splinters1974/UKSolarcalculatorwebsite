Why Commercial Solar

A genuine return, not just a green gesture

Commercial and industrial solar in the UK now offers a compelling business case independent of subsidies. Here's why businesses are acting now.

Lock in energy costs

Grid electricity prices have risen sharply and remain volatile. On-site solar generation provides a hedge against further increases, giving finance teams greater cost certainty.

Meet carbon targets

ESOS, SECR, and supply chain pressure are driving Scope 2 emission reductions. Rooftop solar is the most direct, measurable action most businesses can take.

Mature, proven technology

Solar panels have been commercially deployed for over two decades. Failure rates are low, warranties long, and the UK installer market is competitive and well-established.

Strong, predictable returns

A well-designed commercial system delivers a genuine, measurable ROI over a predictable timeframe. Output can be modelled with confidence before a panel is installed.

Flexible financing options

From capital purchase to Power Purchase Agreements (PPA), lease, or finance packages, there are structures to suit every business — including zero-capex options.

Uses existing roof space

Rooftop and canopy installations require no additional land. Industrial units, warehouses, and retail sites typically have large, south-facing roof areas ideal for solar.

Who We Work With

Solar across every sector

From manufacturing to education, commercial solar makes sense wherever there's a large roof and daytime energy demand.

Manufacturing

High daytime loads, large flat roofs, and significant Scope 2 pressure make manufacturing sites ideal candidates.

Logistics & Warehousing

Vast, south-facing warehouse roofs offer some of the UK's best solar opportunities, with minimal on-site disruption.

Retail & Leisure

Solar generation aligned with trading hours reduces peak grid demand and strengthens sustainability credentials.

Agriculture & Rural

Barn roofs, polytunnel carports, and ground-mounted arrays offer farmers diversified income and reduced energy costs.

Education

Schools and universities benefit from reduced energy bills, curriculum engagement, and demonstrable sustainability leadership.

Healthcare

Predictable operating costs, resilience benefits, and NHS net zero targets make solar a natural fit for health facilities.
